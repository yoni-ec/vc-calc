Portfolio strategy · Monte Carlo model

Scout & convert
vs pure core

An early-stage fund has a fundamental choice: write large checks upfront into a concentrated portfolio, or write many small checks and convert the winners into core-sized positions. This model stress-tests both strategies — and everything in between — using Monte Carlo simulation with power-law exit distributions. Management fees are modeled explicitly: committed capital minus fees equals the investable amount all strategies deploy.

How it works
Three strategies, one fund

All three strategies deploy the same $105M. The question is whether information advantage (scouting small, then doubling down on winners) can outperform upfront ownership concentration.

Strategy A
Pure core
All capital deployed into core-sized checks at a single entry valuation. Maximum ownership per dollar from day one. No scouting, no reserve — just concentrated bets upfront.
Strategy B
Mixed portfolio
12 core checks plus small checks filling the remaining budget, with a $40M reserve for pro-rata follow-ons into winners across both buckets.
Strategy C
Scout + convert
Many small checks at lower valuations. Companies that raise a next round are identified as winners — reserve is deployed to top them up to a full core-sized position at that new valuation.
Model assumptions
What drives the simulation

Each simulation draws exit multiples from a log-normal (power law) distribution — a few huge winners, most companies return nothing. Exit probability varies by scenario. The investable amount (committed capital minus management fees) is what all three strategies deploy.

Key insight behind the scout strategy: small checks enter at $10M valuations (buying ~10% ownership for $1M), while core checks enter at $25M (buying ~16% for $4M). When a scout company raises at $25M, the fund deploys the remaining capital to bring total invested to core-check size — but now owning both the initial seed stake and the new stake, at a cost that reflects real information rather than a blind upfront bet.
